About Guardian

Guardian is a local security dashboard that combines personal breach monitoring with live threat intelligence. It runs on your machine (default: 127.0.0.1) and is designed for individuals and organisations in India who want visibility into data breaches and global security feeds while minimising unnecessary sharing of personal data.

Personal Check queries public breach databases on your behalf. Threat Feed aggregates open-source intelligence (OSINT) from providers such as ThreatFox, URLhaus, and others. Guardian does not sell your digital personal data and does not use it for advertising.

How Guardian compares: Guardian is not a replacement for Have I Been Pwned or VirusTotal — it is a free, local, unified defense dashboard. Breach checks use parallel free sources (XposedOrNot + HackMyIP). IOC scans combine 100k+ local threat feeds with live abuse.ch APIs (ThreatFox, URLhaus, MalwareBazaar). File uploads are scanned locally with ClamAV (when installed) plus hash intelligence — similar to a VirusTotal file check, but without sending your file to the cloud. HIBP is optional only if you explicitly enable it.
Data Fiduciary (operator): Guardian is open-source software you self-host. The operator who deploys this instance acts as the Data Fiduciary under the DPDP Act and should replace the contact details below with their own.

Because Guardian runs locally, most processing of digital personal data occurs on your device. The operator of this deployment acts as a Data Fiduciary (or Data Processor on behalf of your organisation, if applicable). You are the Data Principal when you submit your own identifiers for checking.

Categories of digital personal data

  • Breach checks: Email addresses, phone numbers, or usernames you submit for lookup.
  • Password checks: Only a partial SHA-1 hash fingerprint (k-anonymity) — never your full password.
  • Watchlist: Identifiers you choose to monitor, encrypted at rest on this device (AES-256-GCM).
  • Threat feed: Public threat indicators (IPs, URLs, hashes) — generally not personal data about you.
  • Technical data: Session tokens, encrypted payloads, and optional local UI preferences.

Purpose & lawful processing (DPDP Act)

We process digital personal data for the following purposes:

  • Consent — where you voluntarily submit an identifier for a breach check or add it to a watchlist (Section 6, DPDP Act).
  • Legitimate uses — where permitted, such as responding to a security incident affecting you, or processing necessary for a lawful purpose connected with cybersecurity monitoring you have requested (see Section 7 and applicable notifications under the DPDP Act).
  • Employment / contractual — if your employer or service provider deploys Guardian for workforce security, processing may be under your contract of employment or service agreement.

We process only data adequate and relevant for these purposes and do not retain it longer than necessary (see Retention).

Data processors & cross-border transfers

Breach lookups may contact external APIs (e.g. Have I Been Pwned, XposedOrNot, HackMyIP) over HTTPS. Those providers may process data outside India. Under the DPDP Act, cross-border transfers are permitted to notified countries or under approved contractual mechanisms. Review each provider's terms before enabling live mode. API keys (e.g. HIBP) are read from your local environment only.

Data protection & encryption

Guardian implements defence-in-depth for sensitive personal checks:

  • In transit: X25519 key exchange, HKDF-SHA256 session keys, AES-256-GCM encrypted API payloads between browser and local server. Optional HTTPS/WSS via GUARDIAN_TLS_AUTO.
  • At rest: Watchlist identifiers and breach cache entries sealed with AES-256-GCM. Master key stored at ~/.guardian/master.key (mode 600).
  • Authentication: Session tokens required for API access when cryptography is available.
  • Passwords: Hashed locally; only k-anonymity range queries leave the browser.
Important: Encryption protects data on your machine and over localhost. It does not encrypt data sent to external breach APIs — those providers receive the identifier needed to perform the lookup.

Cookies & local storage

Guardian uses only strictly necessary local storage and session data for security — not third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

  • localStorage: UI preferences (e.g. welcome banner dismissed) on your device only.
  • Session tokens: Held in browser memory for encrypted API calls; not shared with external sites.
  • Server-side files: Encrypted vault files under ~/.guardian/ on your computer.

Under Indian law, including the DPDP Act and IT Rules, processing for cybersecurity and session integrity on a locally hosted tool you operate may fall within permitted purposes when adequate notice is provided (this page). Organisations deploying Guardian to employees should align cookie and storage notices with their internal policies.
